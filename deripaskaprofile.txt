3.1 Oleg Deripaska

Oleg Deripaska is a Russian businessman and industrialist, primarily active in the aluminum, energy, and infrastructure sectors. He is considered a politically exposed person (PEP) due to his significant economic influence and involvement in industries of strategic importance to the Russian economy.

Deripaska is best known as the founder of Basic Element and a major shareholder in UC Rusal, one of the world’s largest aluminum producers. His business activities span multiple sectors, including energy and construction, which are closely linked to national economic priorities. These factors contribute to his classification as a high-risk individual within international compliance frameworks.

Deripaska has been designated under multiple sanctions regimes:

- United States (OFAC) (April 6, 2018)  
- European Union (2022)  
- United Kingdom (2022)  
- Canada and Australia (2022)  

The United States initially designated Deripaska under sanctions targeting individuals associated with the Russian government and key economic sectors. These measures included asset freezes and significant restrictions on business operations. While certain corporate entities linked to Deripaska were later subject to restructuring agreements, the individual designation has remained in place.

Following the escalation of the conflict in Ukraine in 2022, additional sanctions were introduced by the European Union and the United Kingdom. These measures included asset freezes, travel bans, and restrictions on access to international financial systems.

From an analytical perspective, Deripaska represents a characteristic example of an oligarchic PEP, where substantial private wealth intersects with political exposure through involvement in strategic industries. His case also highlights the complexity of sanctions enforcement, particularly in situations where corporate restructuring can partially mitigate restrictions on associated entities, while individual sanctions remain unchanged.

Compared to other oligarchic figures, Deripaska’s profile illustrates how regulatory authorities may differentiate between corporate and individual accountability, reflecting a more nuanced approach within modern AML and sanctions frameworks.
