3.2 Alisher Usmanov

Alisher Usmanov is a Russian-Uzbek businessman and investor, primarily active in the metallurgy, mining, and telecommunications sectors. He is considered a politically exposed person (PEP) due to his significant economic influence and involvement in industries of strategic importance to the Russian economy.

Usmanov has held key positions, including Chairman of Metalloinvest, and has maintained major stakes in companies such as Udokan Copper and MegaFon. His activities in critical sectors contribute to his classification as a high-risk individual within international compliance frameworks.

Usmanov has been designated under multiple sanctions regimes:

- European Union (February 28, 2022)  
- United Kingdom (March 3, 2022)  
- United States (OFAC) (March 3, 2022)  
- Canada (March 14, 2022)  

According to official designations, Usmanov is described as a pro-Kremlin business figure with reported links to senior political leadership. Sanctions introduced by the EU and the UK cite his role in sectors that are considered important to the Russian economy, as well as his position as a beneficiary of the existing economic system.

The United States designated Usmanov under Executive Order 14024 for operating in the metals and mining sector of the Russian Federation economy. These measures resulted in asset freezes, restrictions on financial transactions, and limitations on international business activities.

Sanctions enforcement has also targeted high-value assets reportedly associated with Usmanov, including real estate and maritime property, reflecting a broader approach focused on restricting access to global wealth and financial systems.

From an analytical perspective, Usmanov represents a typical example of an oligarchic PEP, where private wealth intersects with political exposure. His rapid designation across multiple jurisdictions demonstrates a coordinated response among Western sanctions regimes, particularly during the early phase of the 2022 measures.

Compared to political office holders, sanctions applied to Usmanov place a stronger emphasis on private asset restrictions, illustrating differences in how economic and political actors are treated within AML and sanctions frameworks.
