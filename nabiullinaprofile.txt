3.4 Elvira Nabiullina

Elvira Nabiullina is a Russian economist and has served as the Governor of the Central Bank of the Russian Federation since 2013. She is classified as a politically exposed person (PEP) due to her senior role in managing national monetary policy and her influence over the country’s financial system.

Nabiullina has played a key role in shaping Russia’s economic and financial stability, particularly through monetary policy, banking regulation, and currency management. As head of the central bank, she oversees critical financial institutions and is directly involved in decisions affecting inflation, exchange rates, and overall economic resilience.

Nabiullina has been designated under several sanctions regimes:

- United Kingdom (2022)  
- Canada (2022)  
- Australia (2022)  

Unlike some other senior officials, Nabiullina has not been uniformly designated across all major jurisdictions. Notably, the United States and the European Union have taken a more limited or selective approach in applying sanctions to central bank leadership, reflecting the complexity of targeting key financial policymakers.

Sanctions applied to Nabiullina include restrictions on financial interactions and limitations related to international engagement. However, the scope and intensity of these measures differ compared to those imposed on political leaders and oligarchic figures.

From an analytical perspective, Nabiullina represents a technocratic PEP, where risk exposure is associated with institutional responsibility rather than direct political or private economic interests. Her case highlights a more nuanced approach within sanctions frameworks, where policymakers responsible for economic stability may be treated differently from both political leaders and private business actors.

Overall, Nabiullina’s designation pattern illustrates the challenges of balancing financial stability considerations with enforcement objectives in modern AML and sanctions systems.
