3.3 Sergey Lavrov

Sergey Lavrov is a Russian diplomat and has served as the Minister of Foreign Affairs of the Russian Federation since 2004. He is classified as a politically exposed person (PEP) due to his senior position within the government and his direct involvement in shaping and representing state foreign policy.

Lavrov has been a central figure in Russian diplomacy for over two decades, previously serving as the Permanent Representative of Russia to the United Nations. His role places him among the highest-ranking political officials, with significant influence over international relations and strategic decision-making.

Lavrov has been designated under multiple sanctions regimes:

- European Union (February 2022)  
- United Kingdom (February 2022)  
- United States (2022)  
- Canada and other allied jurisdictions (2022)  

These designations were introduced in response to his role as a senior government official associated with the foreign policy actions of the Russian Federation. Sanctions include asset freezes, travel bans, and restrictions on access to international financial systems.

Unlike oligarchic figures, sanctions applied to Lavrov are primarily linked to his formal political position rather than private economic activity. As a result, enforcement measures focus less on asset seizure and more on diplomatic and financial restrictions.

From an analytical perspective, Lavrov represents a high-level political PEP, where risk exposure is directly connected to state authority and decision-making power. His consistent designation across multiple jurisdictions reflects a unified approach toward senior government officials within sanctions frameworks.

Compared to economic actors, Lavrov’s case highlights how sanctions regimes target individuals based on formal political responsibility, illustrating a different dimension of risk classification within AML systems.
